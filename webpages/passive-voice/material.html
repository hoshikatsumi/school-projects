Passive Voice
Present Perfect Tense

What is passive voice?

Passive voice is a grammatical construction in which the subject of the sentence receives the action of the verb, rather than performing the action itself.

In passive voice, the verb is always in the form of a verb to be (am, is, are, was, were, be, been) followed by the past participle (V3) of the main verb.

What is present perfect?

Present perfect tense is used to talk about actions that started in the past and continue to the present, or that have a result in the present. In the passive voice, the present perfect tense is formed using the helping verb "have" or "has" followed by the past participle of the main verb.

Passive voice and active voice are both important tools for writers. Passive voice is often used in academic writing, news articles, business reports, and technical documentation. This is because the passive voice can be used to:

  • Emphasize the action or the object of the sentence rather than the subject.
  • Avoid naming the person or thing that performs the action.
  • Make the writing more formal or objective.

Formula

Affirmative Object + has/have + been + V3 + subject
Negative Object + has/have + not been + V3 + subject
Introgative Has/have + object + been + V3 + object
